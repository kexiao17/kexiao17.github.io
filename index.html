Ke Xiao

I am a first-year Ph.D student in the Information Fusion Lab of Computer Science Department at UMass Amherst, advised by Prof. Madalina Fiterau. My primary research interests resides in the field of Computer Vision, Machine Learning, Artificial Intelligence, and Robotics.

I am currently doing computer vision research on medical imaging datasets, most of which come from UK Biobank. I am developing an automated pipeline consisting of a multi-view CNN-LSTM classification model, U-Net based segmentation model, and a Random Forest ensemble model.

Before coming to UMass, I was working on computer vision research on medical imaging in Priest Lab at Stanford University, supervised/advised by Dr. James R. Priest, Dr. Jason A. Fries. We worked a variety of research projects on cardiac MRI datasets. The work of Aortic Valve Malformation Classification is published at Nature Communications.

Before I ventured into computer vision research, I worked on robotics research at Colorado School of Mines where I graduated with a Master's degree on Robotics, Automation, and Design in Mechanical Engineering department. Beyond the current computer vision research I work on, I still retain my research interests on robotics.

Like my adventuring spirits in research, I like to adventure in my free time as well, which includes surfing, skydiving, scuba diving, hiking, road tripping, and more. When I can not venture far, I like to swim, play guitar, practice yoga, etc.
